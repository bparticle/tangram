## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** Casual puzzlers and design-minded players who appreciate spatial elegance over speed-running. They play in short, unhurried sessions — at a table, on a couch, or between tasks — and enjoy the tactile satisfaction of pieces settling into place.

**Secondary:** Level authors using the `#editor` route to compose silhouettes and publish solvable scrambles.

**Job to be done:** Form a target silhouette by manipulating seven classic pieces under strict contact rules (slide along shared edges, pivot on shared corners), with optional hints and a calm board that only reveals affordances when a piece is selected.

### Brand Personality

**Voice:** Restrained and clear. Rules are stated as laws (一, 二, 三). Copy is precise, never gamified or urgent.

**3-word personality:** Japanese · Minimal · Playful

**Emotional goals:** Zen calm, quiet delight when a piece settles, playful curiosity about how contact unlocks motion. The name **MA** (間) signals negative space and interval — the puzzle is as much about what stays still as what moves.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Japanese minimalism on a physical puzzle table. Warm washi-paper surfaces, vermilion accent, generous negative space. Asymmetric layout: intro column, dominant board, slim tool rail. Dark rules drawer as contrast moment, not a dark-mode product.

**Theme:** Light only — contemplative daytime play.

**Typography:** `Noto Serif JP` for display and body emphasis; `Zen Kaku Gothic New` for UI chrome and labels.

**References:** Traditional tangram craft, Japanese book design (hairline rules, kanji as ornament), zen garden restraint.

**Anti-references:** Generic mobile puzzle apps, neon-on-dark game UI, candy-colored palettes, bounce animations, dashboard card grids, hero metric layouts.

### Design Principles

1. **Calm until contact** — Guides and affordances appear only when the player selects or drags. The board stays still before action.
2. **Physics made legible** — Movement rules are visible on the board (dashed rails, pivot rings), not buried in a manual.
3. **Paper and interval** — Surfaces read as physical (washi, vermilion seal, hairline dividers). Negative space is brand, not filler.
4. **Settle, don't snap** — Motion eases out naturally; pieces seat when essentially correct. Interactions feel weighted, not twitchy.
5. **Playful precision** — Minimal doesn't mean sterile. Kanji, serif headlines, and quiet micro-feedback carry warmth within restraint.
